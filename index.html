This is the timeline of Lionel Messi's achievements and significant events: 1995-2000:Messi plays for the local club team, Newell’s Old Boys, in Rosario, Argentina.
2000-2003:He signs with FC Barcelona and progresses through the club's youth squads. November 16, 2003:Messi makes his team debut as a substitute in a friendly match against FC Porto. October 16, 2004: He officially debuts for FC Barcelona in a match against Espanyol, which Barca wins 1-0. 2007: Messi establishes the Leo Messi Foundation, which focuses on improving access to education and healthcare for children. August 2008: He leads Argentina's soccer team to a gold medal at the Summer Olympics in Beijing. March 11, 2010: Messi is announced as a UNICEF Goodwill Ambassador. 2011-2012 season: He sets the all-time record for most goals scored in a single season for a major European football league, with 73 goals. June 2013: Prosecutors in Barcelona file tax fraud charges against Messi and his father for the period between 2007 and 2009. Messi denies all allegations. June 25, 2013: Messi pays €10 million ($13 million) in taxes to cover the period 2010-2011, but the investigation for alleged tax fraud from 2007 to 2009 continues. August 14, 2013: Messi and his father make a "reparatory" payment of €5 million ($6.6 million) to Spanish authorities for allegedly committing tax fraud between 2007 and 2009. September 27, 2013: Messi and his father testify in a Barcelona court for a preliminary hearing regarding the allegations of defrauding Spanish tax authorities of over $5 million. March 16, 2014: He scores a hat-trick, becoming FC Barcelona's all-time leading scorer with 371 goals, surpassing the previous record set by Paulino.
